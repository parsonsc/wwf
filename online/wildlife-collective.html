Spotlight Project

Project description 1

Helping protect Amur leopards and tigers in the Amur-Heilong, by providing software and training on law enforcement systems that log signs of poaching and help plan anti-poaching patrols effectively.

Project description 2

Training anti-poaching units in Western Ghats, India, and equipping guard posts with essentials like GPS units and equipment to keep in contact with patrols in this important tiger habitat.

Donate to this project Find out more Close

Project description 3

Working with the Kenyan Wildlife Service to monitor and microchip all of Kenya’s black rhinos to help protect them from poachers.

Donate to this project Find out more Close

Project description 4

Working to continue the protection of wildlife in Nepal, following the celebration of two years of ‘zero-poaching’ from 2012-2014 of tigers, elephants and rhinos in the country.

Project in focus: Rhino horn microchipping

Urgent appeal

Join us today to help microchip the last ten rhinos in Lake Nakuru National Park and the Meru Rhino Sanctuary in Kenya.

Rhinos are facing a crisis. There’s been a near eighty-fold increase in in rhino poaching in South Africa in the past seven years. Your support to microchip critically endangered rhinos will allow us to work with the Kenya Wildlife Service to track animals, and help catch poachers should the worst happen.

Project in focus: Stopping the illegal wildlife trade

Match Funded

Join us to stop the demand for illegal wildlife products, including elephant tusks, rhino horns, turtle shells and tiger products.

Increased demand from consumers in many Asian markets for endangered species products has put many animals under increased threat. Your support will help to protect them.

Working with our partners TRAFFIC, the wildlife trade monitoring network.
