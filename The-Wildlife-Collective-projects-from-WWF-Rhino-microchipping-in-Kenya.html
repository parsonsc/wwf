Microchipping rhino horns in Kenya

Join us today and help microchip Kenya’s rhino population.

Rhinos are facing a crisis. There’s been a 7,700% increase in rhino poaching in the past 7seven years. Your support to microchip critically endangered rhinos could allow us to track animals better, and catch poachers should the worst happen.

We're working with our government partner, the Kenyan Wildlife Service (KWS) to support implementation of the national strategy to conserve the black rhino. Between July 2013 to June 2014, 51 rhinos were microchipped in reserves and national parks across Kenya. Right now, you can help protect the remaining rhinos.
